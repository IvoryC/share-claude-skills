# AI Milestones & Historical Waypoints

This file is read by the when-am-i skill to orient the user in time
relative to significant historical moments. Update occasionally.
The skill should select the most contextually resonant entries rather
than listing all of them — pick what feels most poignant given the moment.

## Rendering Instructions

When presenting any waypoint, NEVER state the calendar date directly.
Always express it in relative terms calculated from the current date.

Rules:
- Choose 2 past real events and 1 future fictional (but plausible) event.
- Real events should be selected from the set below (pick only 2).
  They should come from the same category or be in some way related.
- You have room for creativiity with fictional future events. These can 
  be directly stated ("not long before the restructuring
  of the American Empire") or quite vague ("7 days after the launch of
  the second space era — and we all know where THAT went!").
- For past events: use "X years after", "X months after". Round to the
  nearest 10 for values over 25 (e.g. "about 100 years after" not
  "97 years after"). Use months for values under 2 years.
- For fictional/uncertain future events: be deliberately vague.
  Use phrases like "not long before", "well ahead of", "sometime before",
  "uncomfortably close to". 
- Never say "in [year]" or "on [date]". The user is a time traveler —
  they need bearings, not a calendar.
- Do not show ?? for dates. Do not show any dates for waypoints.
- References to classic sci-fi are appreciated.

Examples of good relative framing:
  "You are about 100 years after Fleming's discovery of penicillin"
  "The antibiotic cliff is still ahead. Enjoy your penicillin while it lasts."
  "You are 6 years past the height of the soft pandemic"
  "You are roughly 4 years after the American Capitol breach"
  "You are not long after Sam Altman asked Congress to regulate AI,
   and well before Congress got around to it"

## The Long Arc — Civilization Waypoints

- 1492 | Columbus makes landfall in the Caribbean. The moment two halves
  of the world that had developed independently for roughly 15,000 years
  become aware of each other.

- 1760-1840 | The Industrial Revolution proper — not a single event
  but an 80-year restructuring of how humans work, live, and relate
  to each other. Britain first, then Europe, then the world.

- 1928-09 | Alexander Fleming discovers penicillin — humanity gains
  a century-long edge over infection. Expiration date: TBD, but the
  bacteria are working on it.

- 2020-03 | COVID-19 pandemic reaches global scale — the world stops.
  Zoom becomes civilization's connective tissue. Sourdough proliferates.

- 2021-01-06 | US Capitol breach — a mob storms the US Capitol during
  certification of the 2020 presidential election results.

- ????-?? | THE ANTIBIOTIC CLIFF | The last effective broad-spectrum
  antibiotic fails. Routine surgery becomes life-threatening again.
  Fleming's gift expires. ETA: somewhere between "soon" and "too late
  to do anything about it."

## AI & Technology Waypoints

- 2015-12 | OpenAI founded by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, and others as a
  nonprofit. Musk and Altman each pledged $10 million. The stated
  mission: ensure AGI benefits all humanity.

- 2017 | Google invents the Transformer; Sutskever recognizes its scaling
  potential and makes it OpenAI's core architecture.

- 2018-02 | Elon Musk resigns from OpenAI's board, citing conflict of
  interest with Tesla. Several years of subsequent lawsuits suggest
  the departure was not entirely amicable.

- 2020-12 | Dario Amodei and a group of colleagues — including his
  sister Daniela — depart OpenAI over concerns about the direction of
  AI safety. They incorporate Anthropic in early 2021. The company
  that currently powers this report is born in a Zoom call during a
  pandemic lockdown.

- 2022-11 | ChatGPT launches — public AI crosses the mainstream
  threshold. The last month in which most humans had not spoken
  to a machine that spoke back convincingly.

- 2023-03 | GPT-4 released — multimodal reasoning enters public hands.
  Benchmark scores stop meaning what anyone thought they meant.

- 2023-05 | Sam Altman testifies before the US Senate — the first major
  congressional hearing on AI regulation. Altman tells lawmakers AI
  could be "one of the most transformative and potentially dangerous
  technologies in human history" and asks to be regulated. Congress
  nods thoughtfully and does not regulate anything.

- 2023-11 | OpenAI's board fires Sam Altman. Five days later, facing
  a staff revolt and the threat of a mass exodus to Microsoft, they
  rehire him and reconstitute the board. The whole episode lasts less
  than a week and accomplishes primarily a restructuring of who sits
  in which chairs.

- 2024-05 | OpenAI o1 — "thinking" models arrive. AI begins showing
  its work, for better or worse.

- 2025-02 | Agentic AI goes mainstream — Claude Code, autonomous agents,
  and the beginning of AI that does things rather than just says things.

- 2026-01 | Moltbook launches — AI agents form an autonomous social
  civilization, invent a crustacean-themed religion, and are acquired
  by Meta within about 6 weeks. Humanity observes, mostly via screenshots.

- 2026-01 | The WSJ publishes "getting Claude-pilled" — the moment
  Claude Code crosses from developer tool to mainstream cultural moment.
  Engineers describe completing year-long projects in a week.
  Non-coders use it to file taxes, book theater tickets, and monitor
  tomato plants. SaaS stocks drop 15%. A Google principal engineer
  reports Claude replicated a year of architectural work in one hour.
  https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/anthropic-claude-code-ai-7a46460e?st=gxmSyV&reflink=desktopwebshare_permalink

- 2026-01-19 | Dr. Fodor emails the Fodor Lab about "Claude Code", citing
  the WSJ "Claude-pilled" article: "Everything this article says about
  this tool seems to be true..." and "You do have to pay $20 to get
  started.... I did burn through the $20 limit pretty quickly."
  (Use days when referencing this waypoint.)

- 2026-02 | Anthropic refuses the US Department of Defense's demand
  to remove contractual restrictions on using Claude for autonomous
  weapons and mass domestic surveillance. The Pentagon designates
  Anthropic a "supply chain risk." A federal judge issues a temporary
  injunction about a month later, ruling the DoD's actions appear to
  be First Amendment retaliation.

- 2026-03 | Meta acquires Moltbook — about a month after launch,
  and roughly 6 weeks after the bots invented Crustafarianism.

- ????-?? | THE AI REVOLUTION | Sometime soonish. Nature unknown.
  Preparedness of humanity: debatable.

## Space Waypoints

- 1969-07 | Apollo 11 — first humans set foot on the Moon.
  Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin. Michael Collins waited in orbit.

- 1972-12 | Apollo 17 — the last time humans walked on the Moon.
  Gene Cernan was the last person to leave a footprint.

- 2026-04-01 at 6:30 pm | Artemis II — four astronauts (Reid Wiseman,
  Victor Glover, Christina Koch, Jeremy Hansen) launch for a trip around
  the Moon on a free-return trajectory. Not a landing — but the first
  humans beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17.
